ThingsHack: The IOT Hackathon
EVENT DATE :
3rd FEB 2018
EVENT TIME :
2:30 A.M.
EVENT VENUE :

ABOUT THE EVENT
  • The multiple, overlapping technology layers involved in building a smarter and connected world make it necessary to bring together diverse sets of thinkers, makers, and coders from different fields.

    We are organizing an IoT hackathon to allow you to push the boundaries of creativity, innovation, reality, and tech to build solutions and concepts that have the potential to make a difference in people’s lives.

    We are inviting teams of makers, developers, and technologists to collaborate and explore the next-gen technologies of smart homes, smart wearables, smart cities, and connected cars / smart driving experience in an environment that encourages you innovate and build

    Form teams, explore possibilities, and pitch your idea with a team of at most 4 members with a working model/prototype. The judges will screen the ideas based on factors like innovation, creativity, social impact, and feasibility. The idea should be original and concrete. Every participant will get a participation certificate and top 3 winners will get cash prices and certificate. Also, the idea can be picked and incubated to form a new startup by a visiting company "Busineers".


    Here is your big opportunity to build something that the world has never seen before.
TOPICS
  • Home automation
  • Smart city
  • Smart driving / Connected cars
  • Smart Wearables
  • Open Category(any topic relating to IOT)
RULES & REGULATIONS
  • Team can have at most 4 members.
  • You must use either Raspberry pi or Arduino for your model.
  • It must be a working prototype/model.
  • A presentation must be prepared for 3 mins - 10 mins, a power point presentation can also be used to explain the working, impact and others details about the project.
  • Entry fees of ₹100/head will be charged and each member will be given a hard copy of participation certificate signed by the authority. (ex if team has 4 members, charge will be ₹400)
  • On-spot registrations are also allowed.
